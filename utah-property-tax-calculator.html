Utah Property Tax — FAQ

The average effective property tax rate in Utah is 0.52%. Salt Lake County has a rate of 0.56%. Rates vary by county, city, and school district.
In Utah, property taxes are typically due: November 30. Check with your local county assessor for the exact deadlines in your area, as dates can vary by municipality.
Utah does not have a statewide homestead exemption, but may offer other relief programs for seniors, veterans, and low-income homeowners.
You can lower your Utah property tax by: (1) applying for all exemptions you qualify for, (2) appealing your property assessment if it seems too high, (3) checking for senior, veteran, or disability programs in your county.
This calculator uses the 2026 average effective rate for Utah (0.52%) and applies standard homestead exemptions. It gives a reliable estimate. For your exact tax bill, contact the Salt Lake County assessor's office or visit your county's official website.
