States With Highest and Lowest Property Taxes in 2026

From New Jersey's eye-watering 2.23% to Hawaii's barely-there 0.26% — here is the complete 2026 ranking of all 50 states by effective property tax rate.

The Complete 2026 State Rankings

Property tax rates vary enormously across the US — from less than 0.3% in Hawaii to over 2.2% in New Jersey. Understanding where your state ranks helps you plan, budget, and know when to appeal your assessment.

Top 10 Highest Property Tax States (2026)

  1. New Jersey — 2.23% — Highest in the nation. Average annual bill: $9,388 on median home.
  2. Illinois — 2.07% — Second highest. Chicago metro drives rates up significantly.
  3. Connecticut — 1.79% — No statewide homestead exemption compounds the impact.
  4. New Hampshire — 1.77% — No income or sales tax; property taxes fund everything.
  5. Vermont — 1.73% — High rates, though income-based relief is available.
  6. Texas — 1.60% — No income tax but property taxes are the tradeoff.
  7. Nebraska — 1.49% — High rates even outside Omaha and Lincoln.
  8. Wisconsin — 1.51% — Milwaukee County reaches 2.17%.
  9. Michigan — 1.44% — Wayne County (Detroit) hits 1.87%.
  10. Iowa — 1.43% — Above-average rates with limited exemptions.
💡 High tax rate does not always mean high tax bill. Texas has a 1.60% rate but median home values are lower than California (0.71%), so the actual dollar amount can be similar.

Top 10 Lowest Property Tax States (2026)

  1. Hawaii — 0.26% — Lowest in the nation. High home values, low rates.
  2. Alabama — 0.41% — Very low rates, strong homestead programs.
  3. Nevada — 0.44% — Tax abatement cap keeps increases minimal.
  4. Idaho — 0.46% — Growing state with still-low property taxes.
  5. Tennessee — 0.48% — No income tax and low property taxes.
  6. Colorado — 0.49% — TABOR amendment limits tax growth.
  7. Arizona — 0.51% — Assessed at only 10% of full cash value.
  8. Louisiana — 0.51% — $75,000 homestead exemption helps significantly.
  9. South Carolina — 0.52% — Owner-occupied rate of 4% assessment ratio.
  10. Utah — 0.52% — Low rates despite high home values in Salt Lake.

What Drives High Property Tax Rates?

States with high property tax rates typically share one or more of these traits: no income tax (Texas, New Hampshire), heavy reliance on local school funding, older housing stock requiring more services, or high population density with expensive infrastructure.
