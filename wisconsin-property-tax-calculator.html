About Wisconsin Property Tax

Homestead credit for low-income residents. School levy credit reduces taxes on owner-occupied homes.

  • Milwaukee County rate: 2.17%
  • Homestead exemption: Not available statewide

How Wisconsin Property Tax is Calculated

Wisconsin Property Tax — FAQ

The average effective property tax rate in Wisconsin is 1.51%. Milwaukee County has a rate of 2.17%. Rates vary by county, city, and school district.
In Wisconsin, property taxes are typically due: January 31. Check with your local county assessor for the exact deadlines in your area, as dates can vary by municipality.
Wisconsin does not have a statewide homestead exemption, but may offer other relief programs for seniors, veterans, and low-income homeowners.
You can lower your Wisconsin property tax by: (1) applying for all exemptions you qualify for, (2) appealing your property assessment if it seems too high, (3) checking for senior, veteran, or disability programs in your county.
This calculator uses the 2026 average effective rate for Wisconsin (1.51%) and applies standard homestead exemptions. It gives a reliable estimate. For your exact tax bill, contact the Milwaukee County assessor's office or visit your county's official website.
