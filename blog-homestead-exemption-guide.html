Homestead Exemption: Complete Guide for 2026

A homestead exemption can save you hundreds or even thousands of dollars per year on property taxes. Here is everything you need to know for 2026.

What Is a Homestead Exemption?

A homestead exemption reduces the taxable value of your primary residence for property tax purposes. Instead of paying tax on the full assessed value of your home, you pay on the assessed value minus the exemption amount.

Example: Your home is assessed at $300,000. Your state has a $50,000 homestead exemption. You pay property tax on $250,000 — not $300,000.

Which States Have the Best Homestead Exemptions?

  • Texas — $100,000 off school district taxes (2026)
  • California — Up to $300,000 for qualifying seniors
  • Louisiana — $75,000 off assessed value
  • Hawaii — $100,000 for owner-occupants in Honolulu
  • Florida — $50,000 plus Save Our Homes assessment cap

How to Apply for a Homestead Exemption

  1. Contact your county property appraiser or assessor's office
  2. Confirm your eligibility (primary residence, ownership status)
  3. Complete the application form (usually one page)
  4. Submit proof of residency (driver's license, utility bills)
  5. Deadline varies by state — most are January 1 to April 30

Senior Homestead Exemptions

Most states offer enhanced exemptions for homeowners 65 and older. These can exempt additional amounts from taxable value or freeze your assessment at its current level so it never increases.
