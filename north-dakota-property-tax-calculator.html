About North Dakota Property Tax

Homestead credit for seniors and disabled: reduces assessed value by up to $9,000.

  • Average effective rate: 0.89%
  • Payment dates: March 1
  • Cass County rate: 0.97%
  • Homestead exemption: $9,000 off assessed value

How North Dakota Property Tax is Calculated

Your North Dakota property tax bill = Assessed Value × Tax Rate.

If you qualify for the homestead exemption, your assessed value is reduced by $9,000 before the rate is applied, saving you up to $80/year.

Rates vary by county and school district. Cass County has an effective rate of 0.97%, while the statewide average is 0.89%.

North Dakota Property Tax — FAQ

The average effective property tax rate in North Dakota is 0.89%. Cass County has a rate of 0.97%. Rates vary by county, city, and school district.
In North Dakota, property taxes are typically due: March 1. Check with your local county assessor for the exact deadlines in your area, as dates can vary by municipality.
